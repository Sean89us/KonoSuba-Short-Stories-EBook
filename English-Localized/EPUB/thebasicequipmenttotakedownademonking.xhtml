The Basic Equipment to Take Down a Demon King

Translator: Cannongerbil

Editors: Ulti

Localization: Sean92us (Utilizing GPT-5.2)

Occurrence: After Volume 15

I turned my sword over in my hand and muttered to myself.

“…I really want a magic sword.”

Yeah. A magic sword.

Even after taking down more than a few of the Demon King’s generals, my gear was still pretty pathetic.

If this were a game, I would’ve looted some rare equipment off a boss by now—or found some ridiculous treasure stashed in their fortress. But no. Nothing like that had happened at all.

…Right then, Megumin glanced up from her book, “How to Prepare Beef and Potato Stew for Dummies,” and said,

“Isn’t your Chunchunmaru already a pretty rare sword? What’s so unsatisfactory about it?”

“The name, for starters.”

I’d had it made using half-remembered Japanese sword-forging trivia from documentaries and whatever else I’d seen.

It wasn’t a proper katana, and it had a painfully embarrassing name engraved right into the blade.

“This might sound boastful, but there aren’t many swords with a name that cool, you know. I’ve told you before— your naming sense is really strange.”

“I never thought I’d live to see the day you criticized my naming sense…”

Where does this girl even get that kind of confidence?

“Still, a magic sword, huh? I get why you’d want one. It’d be hard to take down the Demon King without a powerful magic weapon, after all.”

“I’m not doing this for some grand destiny, so knock it off.”

Even so, there are plenty of monsters in this world you can’t hurt without a magic weapon.

And at this point, I’m a pretty well-known adventurer.

Maybe it really was time to get my hands on at least one decent magic weapon.

“…All right. Let’s go get you a magic sword.”

Megumin said it in the exact same tone someone would use for, ‘I’m going to pick up milk.’

Not long after, we arrived at the adventurers’ guild.

“See? There’s a magic sword.”

“It’s been a while… but what is this about?”

Standing in front of us was the Magic Sword Guy.

“Hey, Megumin. If I take the magic sword away from Magic Sword Guy, doesn’t he just become… Regular Guy?”

“It’s true. That magic sword really is his only defining feature…”

“Hey, wait! I have plenty of other memorable traits! And would you please call me by my name?!”

The Magic Sword Guy started making a scene for no reason.

“You’re going to bother everyone if you keep shouting, Magic Sword Guy. Anyway, Kazuma wants a magic sword. If I remember right, you have a powerful one called Gram, don’t you?”

“Why can you remember my sword’s name but not mine?!”

He was still just as agitated.

Something occurred to me.

“Megumin, Gram’s a magic sword that only he can use. I don’t think I could make proper use of it.”

“Is that so? It really is a waste for Magic Sword Guy to have a weapon that powerful when he can’t even manage to take down a single Demon King’s general…”

His magic sword is a divine treasure linked to him alone.

“A-A waste… Hey, wait! I’ve taken down a few high-bounty targets!”

“…Well, fine. In that case, let’s head to our next stop.”

“Listen to me!”

And Megumin’s “next stop” was…

“Welcome, dear customers in search of a magic sword. Vanir stocks all kinds of magic weapons, so feel free to choose whatever you like.”

“Ah, right. If you want a magic sword, a magic-item shop is a pretty obvious place to start.”

Vanir greeted us cheerfully as we stepped into Wiz’s magic-item shop.

“Kazuma, look! This is a really powerful magic item. And I can tell —it’s compatible with me!”

“Oh? As expected of a Crimson Demon, your eye for magic items is sharp. That is a rare magic sword that explodes when you pour mana into it.”

“I’m not buying that.”

Based on past experience, Vanir would absolutely try to pawn some useless junk off on me, so I got ahead of it and pulled out a small, heavy bag of coins.

“Use your powers and find me a magic sword I can actually use. And no trash.”

“Leave it to me, dear customer! Fuhahahahaha! Behold, my powers of foresight…!”

After taking the bag, Vanir’s eyes began to glow…

“…Brat. Magic swords tend to choose their own wielders…”

“Don’t say another word! You’re about to tell me there isn’t a magic sword I can use, aren’t you?!”

Great. This again. The downsides of having the weakest class were coming back to haunt me—again!

But Vanir shook his head and handed me a small knife case from the shelf.

“Isn’t this a cooking knife?”

Inside was a fish filleting knife.

“This is the magic item ‘Fish Slayer.’ It is enchanted to deal extra damage to aquatic creatures. Vanir wholeheartedly recommends it.”

“You bastard, are you telling me to pick a fight with some powerful enemy armed with a kitchen knife?! I’m not going into battle with a kitchen knife! …But it does look kind of cool, so I’ll buy it anyway!”

And that’s how my kitchen ended up equipped with a magic sword.
