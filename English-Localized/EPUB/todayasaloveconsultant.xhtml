Consulting with this Masked Devil! - Today as a Love Consultant

Fanfic

Translator: yuNS

Occurrence: Fanfic (non-canon)

Localization: Sean92us (Utilizing GPT-5.2)

A little after lunch, once the guild’s lunchtime rush started to thin—

“To think you’d come to Vanir for advice. How rare. How delightfully absurd, O Crimson Demon.”

“Who are you calling absurd!? Even I have problems I want to talk to someone about, okay!?”

A girl from that famously over-the-top tribe—the Crimson Demons—had come to consult me.

Her crimson eyes glittering, she planted herself in the chair across from my counter and tried very hard to look serious.

“I heard your fortune-telling is scarily accurate, so… I want your help with something.”

“I don’t mind, so long as you pay. Now then—what do you wish to know? The best locations for casting Explosion magic, perhaps?”

“Not that! I mean, I do want to know, but that’s not why I’m here today. I want to know the true identity of a certain someone.”

A certain someone?

“Every time I see him, he’s wearing a mask, and he has this… ‘shadowy hero’ vibe. He’s really cool, but—how do I put this…? Ever since the first time I saw him, I’ve had this nagging feeling he isn’t a stranger. It’s been bothering me. No matter what, I want to see his real face.”

“So. You desire to see Vanir’s true face beneath this mask.”

“No! Why would I want that!? I mean the true face of a certain thieving group!”

A thieving group?

“Ah. The wanted thief with taste, the one who wears a mask much like mine. The guild staff have asked me the same question before. But for reasons even I find irritating… I cannot get a clear read on that one.”

“How disappointing. You act all smug and confident, but when it matters, you’re just plain useless.”

“To be called useless by someone whose usefulness peaks once per day. Even I did not foresee such an insult.”

“Hey! Don’t say it like the only thing I can do is Explosion! …But still, what a shame. If I could meet that masked thief again, I’d like to—uh—get that cool ma…sk…”

Halfway through the word, she froze like she’d just cast a spell on herself.

“That’s it! The mask! I’ve wanted one for ages! I can buy one at Wiz’s shop, right?”

“It warms my heart to see such appreciation for my craftsmanship. However, ‘Vanir Masks’ sold out long ago. To preserve their exquisite exclusivity, I have chosen not to restock for the time being.”

“What!? Don’t be stingy—just give me one! You were bragging two seconds ago! Come on!”

“Y-You brat—unhand me! Are you attempting to kidnap a devil!? There will be a restock someday, so be patient and wait!”

The Crimson Demon grabbed at my mask and tried to pry it off my face.

I peeled her fingers away and shooed her back, and that was when she suddenly clapped her hands like she’d just had a revelation.

“Wait, the mask! That thief was wearing one you made! If that’s the case, doesn’t that mean the thief is one of your customers?”

“Hmph. Everyone who purchased a Vanir Mask was, in their own way, a little strange. But I do not recall any of them being a master thief of that caliber…”

From what I’d heard, the masked thief had slipped through the royal palace’s tight security, dropped a number of knights in an instant, humbled a fairly famous adventurer, and then walked away with a treasure.

It pained me to admit it, but I had no memory of handing one of my masks to someone that outstanding.

“Most likely, the mask changed hands. Still… a name like ‘Masked Thief Brigade’ shows impeccable taste. I would very much enjoy a conversation with them someday.”

“If that ever happens, call me too. All right, I’m going—I still have my routine work to do today.”

She stood, and as she turned to leave, I let my voice drift after her.

“Are you quite certain you have nothing else to ask? Surely there’s something you’re truly worried about.”

She paused at the doorway, spun around, and pointed at me.

“Right! You still haven’t told me the best places to cast Explosion magic!”

She marched back and sat down again, but that was not what I meant.

“Such trifles are of no consequence. More important—would you like me to divine the future of the man who has taken up residence in your heart?”

“!”

At my words, she visibly jolted.

“His future is not yet decided. How fascinating… I would never have guessed. That man is far more popular than he appears at first glance.”

“W-What are you talking about!? Even if you try to bait me like that, I won’t fall for it! …But, um, I guess it’s fine if I listen a little. Just a little, okay?”

“Then you must pay to hear what comes next.”

…Hearing that, the Crimson Demon opened her purse and stared down into it for an uncomfortably long time.

Then, with all the drama of a grand transaction, she carefully produced its contents and placed them in my hand.

“I’m afraid I cannot accept ‘almost-filled’ stamp cards and coupons as currency.”

Her face flushed bright red. Then she snapped her chin up, as if she could intimidate reality itself into changing.

“My name is Megumin! I am Axel’s number-one mage, and I forge my own destiny as I walk a thorny path! There’s no way I have a man in my heart—and there’s no way I’d consult you about stupid girly feelings!”

With that declaration, she bolted out of the guild.

The flustered feelings of a young girl—feelings that made even the most jaded onlooker smile—were, to a devil like me, truly exquisite negative emotions.
