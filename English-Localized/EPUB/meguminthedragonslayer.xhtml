Megumin the Dragon Slayer

Translator: Cannongerbil

Editors: Ulti, Xenthur, Keel the swift

Localization: Sean92us (Utilizing GPT-5.2)

Occurrence: After Volume 1

At a quarry just outside town.

“All right, young lady—I’m counting on you.”

“Heh, leave it to me. Feast your eyes—I shall show you a glimpse of my true power! If I got serious, this entire quarry would…! Ah, could you stand a little farther back? Yeah, that’ll do.”

The foreman and Megumin were discussing the plan for the blast.

However…

“Why do I have to come along?”

“I don’t have a choice. After I use my magic, I won’t be able to move, so I need you to carry me home.”

And for some reason, I got dragged out to the quarry, too.

“You don’t need me for that. You could just have the foreman carry you back. He’s probably stronger than I am.”

“What kind of nonsense is that? Look at him—he’s huge and built like a beast. If I were left alone with him while I couldn’t move, who knows what he’d do to a delicate girl like me?”

“That is not going to happen!”

“Hey! Why are you two yelling!? Explain it to me—slowly!”

Megumin raised her staff, her eyes glowing red. Seeing that, the foreman hastily said,

“W-Well, setting that aside, our problem is that giant boulder. We can’t get any work done with it in the way. I talked it over with the guild, and they recommended you. So—what do you say? Think you can handle it?”

“Of course! My name is Megumin! The one who will someday slay the Demon King! A rock like that is nothing compared to—”

“Pii~! Pii~!”

….?

A bird-like cry suddenly rang out, and we exchanged confused looks.

When I looked closer at the boulder, I could make out something like a nest perched on top.

“Oh, that’s a wyvern! A wyvern hatchling! Good grief—nesting this close to town… But it’s good you’re here, young lady. Blow it away along with that rock. It may be a hatchling, but wyverns are aggressive carnivores. If we leave it be, it’ll cause serious damage later. Its parents aren’t here, so this is our best chance to deal with it.”

“Ah!”

Hearing the engineer’s words, Megumin let out a squeak.

Megumin stared at the small wyvern as it kept chirping.

It was probably hard for her to finish off such a defenseless creature, but as adventurers, we can’t let dangerous monsters roam free.

And this wasn’t just any monster—it was a wyvern. Once it grew up, it would definitely become a serious threat to people.

But Megumin…

“U..uuu…”

…She wore a conflicted expression and didn’t move.

In the end, Megumin chose not to cast her spell, and she lowered her head dejectedly.

Seeing her, the engineer scratched his head and smiled.

“Hah, well, I guess it can’t be helped! It’s probably too cruel a job for a young lady. I’ll call you back to deal with that boulder after the wyvern leaves the nest!”

The foreman let out a hearty laugh.

Megumin seemed to brighten up upon hearing his words.

“Let’s take it down in a proper battle once it’s grown up.”

I said with a smile.

“Still, you missed out on a great opportunity, young lady. It may be small, but if you killed it, you’d still be a wyvern slayer. And wyverns are a kind of dragon, so you’d even be able to call yourself a dragon slayer.”

The engineer joked.

I joined in his laughter.

“You can call yourself a dragon slayer even if you beat a hatchling? That’s pretty nice, isn’t it? It definitely sounds like the kind of title a hero who defeats the Demon King would have—”

“Explosion-!”

Megumin cut me off with her spell.

At the same time, a loud boom rocked the quarry, followed by a strong shockwave.

The sudden spell gave us no time to brace ourselves, and the foreman and I both went tumbling.

Flat on the ground, I shifted to look around.

The foreman must have hit his head—his eyes had rolled back, and he looked completely out of it.

On the other side, Megumin had collapsed after spending all her magic. Without even lifting her head, she said proudly,

“My name is Megumin! Wielder of explosion magic, and the one who defeated a dragon! Kazuma, from now on, call me ’Dragon Slayer Megumin.‘”

“You little… Can’t you just let things end nicely for once!?”
