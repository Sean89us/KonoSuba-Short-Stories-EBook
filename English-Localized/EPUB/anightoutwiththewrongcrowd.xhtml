A Night Out with the Wrong Crowd

Translator: Cannongerbil

Editors: Striker

Localization: Sean92us (Utilizing GPT-5.2)

Occurrence: Volume 17

“Thank you for attending my daughter’s birthday party. She’s always looked up to you, Lady Dustiness, so I’m sure this was the best party she’s ever had.”

The host of tonight’s party, the marquis, said as he saw us to the door.

“It was my pleasure. I’m jealous—you have such a beautiful, adorable young lady. If I ever get the chance…”

I smiled at the young girl peeking out from behind the marquis and gave her a wave.

After saying my farewells, I headed for the carriage—

“Lady Dustiness, are you heading back already? The party had to end early because the marquis’s daughter is so young, but isn’t it a little early for us adults to turn in?”

“If you have time, perhaps you’d like to join us for a few drinks. Of course, it wouldn’t just be men—there will be noble ladies as well. Everyone would be delighted if you came.”

Just as I was about to step into the carriage, a nobleman a little older than me called out.

Behind him were several other noblemen watching me with fond smiles.

“…No, I’m sorry, but if I’m late getting back, everyone at home will worry. Perhaps another time—”

“I-Is that so? N-no, I’m the one holding you up. Please forgive me!”

“The Dustiness family handles much of the realm’s internal security, so of course you’re busy! Please, don’t worry about it!”

When I replied with a smile, the two men blushed bright red… and let out small sighs.

Socializing is important, but I could already tell how the rest of the night would go: smiling nobles surrounding me and showering me with praise.

I’d had enough of playing the role of “proper noblewoman” for one night.

With one last goodbye, I climbed into the carriage—

“…I’m home…”

“Waaaaah! Darkness!”

The moment I opened the mansion’s front door, Aqua came rushing at me in tears.

I could guess why she was crying, but…

“What’s with this the moment I walk in? Did Kazuma scold you again?”

“No, listen! Kazuma’s awful! He snuck off to that new pub—the one we promised we’d visit together—without me!”

“Hey, I didn’t sneak anywhere! First of all, we never promised we’d go together! And if a new pub opens in town, of course I’m going to check it out!”

As it turned out, it was for a much pettier reason than I’d expected.

“…Then why don’t you two just go together? We could invite Megumin too…”

“Hah!? Even if you take me, you won’t let me drink anyway, right? So I don’t really… Fine, I’m coming. I’m coming for the food.”

Megumin grumbled as she headed off to get ready.

“…Huh? It’s a little late to ask, but isn’t that a really expensive dress? Did you go to some noble’s party or something?”

It really was a little late, but Kazuma asked anyway.

“Hey, Darkness, you didn’t ditch us to go to some glamorous noble party and hog all the good food for yourself, did you!? Did you bring anything back? You brought a box stuffed full of delicious food, right!?”

“S-Stop it, Aqua. Don’t pull on my dress! This kind of outfit slips off really easily! And how would I do that at a birthday party for a noble girl!? Kazuma, Megumin—say something to her!”

“Aqua, do your best! If you make her flash us, I’ll treat you to something expensive at the pub!”

“This man is the worst! Darkness, hurry up and change! Then we’ll go out for a night on the town!”

Saying that, Megumin started wrestling with Aqua.

I’d already eaten, so I’d planned to spend the rest of the night at home…

However—

“We eat together at the Adventurers’ Guild all the time, but it’s rare for us to spend a night out like this. Hey, Aqua—why don’t you teach this young lady some games? Then when you stumble home in the morning after being out all night, Darkness might be a little gentler when she lectures you.”

“Oh, that’s an interesting idea. Very well! I, Aqua—the famous bar-hopping goddess of this town—will teach you how to hold your liquor at this new pub!”

“It’s not fair if everyone but me gets to drink! I’m drinking tonight too!”

Still—

“…All right. I’ll change—give me a moment.”

“Oh, and dress modestly tonight. If you show up in your usual, uh, erotic outfit, some drunk guy is going to come over and start trying to buy you.”

“W-What do you mean, my usual erotic outfit!? I dress modestly!”

When I shot back, all three of them froze and looked at me like I was speaking another language.

Yeah. Tonight—

“I’m tired of being formal and playing the noblewoman! Let’s cut loose tonight!”

Leaving the three of them looking even more dumbfounded, I went to my room in high spirits and changed quickly—
