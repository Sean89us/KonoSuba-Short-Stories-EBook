The Strongest Devil Summoner of the Crimson Demons

Translator: Cannongerbil

Editors: Ulti

Localization: Sean92us (Utilizing GPT-5.2)

Occurrence: Volume 14

After we got to the Crimson Demon Village, I had way too much time on my hands.

There’s not much to do in a tiny village out in the sticks.

Aqua and Darkness ran off somewhere, and Megumin dumped Komekko on me before disappearing into her chores.

So Komekko and I ended up in the backyard together.

“—Right, come play with Big Bro.”

“Okay. Big Sis told me to play with Big Bro too.”

That girl… she’d better remember this.

…Oh, I thought of something. This would be devastating for someone as weirdly attached to her sister as Megumin.

I pulled out the snacks I’d brought as a gift.

“Hey, Komekko, between me and Megumin, who do you like more? I’ll give you this snack if you—”

“I like Big Bro more than Big Sis.”

Watching Komekko snatch the snack without a second thought, I briefly wondered if this was really okay.

“A-Ah, never mind. Say that exact line to Megumin when she comes back, and I’ll give you another snack.”

“Okay!”

Good girl.

Trying not to laugh, I crouched down to Komekko’s height.

“All right, what should we play? House? Tag?”

“Devil summoning.”

…Is that why they’re called Crimson Demons?

“Yeah, let’s save that for when you’re older, okay? You’re a Crimson Demon, so it’d be a real headache if you actually managed to call up a real devil.”

“Okay. I got scolded by a little goblin when I summoned him without an offering.”

“Megumin! Megumin! Get over here, now!”

I yelled out in the direction of the house.

“—Seriously, what’s the matter? I told you to keep Komekko company while I did the housework, didn’t I?”

Megumin ran over, wiping her hands on her apron.

“Now’s not the time for that. Your sister did something amazing. Hey, Komekko, tell Megumin what you just told me.”

“I like Big Bro more than Big Sis.”

“What are you saying!? Komekko, I’ll apologize if I did anything bad! It really hurts, so please stop!”

No, not that line.

“I said it, so give me another snack.”

“Kazuma, I’d like a word with you. Come with me.”

“That’s not it! I mean, yeah, I bribed her to say it, but that’s not why I called you. Your sister summoned a devil!”

I rattled that off while handing the snack to Komekko.

Megumin just sighed.

“That’s something every Crimson Demon does at some point. When I was little, I used to perform the ritual to summon the God of Destruction.”

“Don’t say that with that nostalgic look! …Wait, what!? You’re saying it’s fine!? But Komekko said something about a little goblin…”

Megumin chuckled, probably amused by how panicked I looked.

“Summoning devils requires an extremely difficult, delicate ritual. And even if you have the talent, it takes at least a decade of work to summon even an imp—the weakest kind of devil.”

“Seriously?”

But if that was true, then what was Komekko talking about…?

“Besides, even if the ritual did work, a goblin isn’t a devil, so there’s no way one would be summoned. Komekko, don’t tease him too much, okay?”

“Okay.”

“All right, I’m going back to my chores. You two play together, okay?”

After patting Komekko on the head, Megumin went back into the house.

…So she was just messing with me…

I’d thought I’d stumbled into yet another incident, but apparently it was just my imagination.

Seriously, these two sisters never stop throwing me for a loop.

“See? Your sister got mad because you said stuff like that. It’s a little hypocritical coming from me, but lying is bad, okay?”

“I didn’t lie.”

That stubborn streak was just like her sister’s.

“But Megumin just said it, didn’t she? You can’t summon a goblin with a summoning ritual.”

“But it came out. I offered it a cicada shell, but it scolded me. ‘Don’t call upon me without a proper offering. I’ll let you off this time, but don’t do it again,’ it said.”

…

That bad feeling came rushing back.

“Hey. Can you describe that ‘goblin’ for me?”

Just in case, I checked with Komekko…

“It was really small and black, and it had claws, wings, and a tail.”

“Megumin! Get over here, now!”
