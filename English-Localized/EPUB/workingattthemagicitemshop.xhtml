Dust Spinoff 5 Short Story 2: Working at the Magic Item Shop

Translator: Cannongerbil

Localization: Sean92us (Utilizing GPT-5.2)

Occurrence: Dust Spinoff Volume 5

“Thanks for having me today.”

“Thank you very much, Dust.”

I woke up early and dragged myself to the magic item shop, where the beautiful shopkeeper, Wiz, greeted me.

“U-Um, I really think it’s best not to hire Dust, but…”

Standing beside Wiz’s gentle smile and saying something that rude was the Loli Succubus—still as flat as ever.

“Quit saying unnecessary things. This doesn’t have anything to do with you.”

“True, it might not be my problem, but I have to protect this store while Vanir’s away.”

Get passionate about whatever you want, but at this point you’re basically just squatting here.

“It’s fine. Usually nothing much happens here, so there shouldn’t be any problems. And it’s nice to have a guy around to lend a hand.”

“Good. I needed something easy. I can’t work at the guild for three days, so this really helps me out.”

“Uugh… I can’t shake the feeling this is a bad idea…”

“No need to worry. I’m the type of guy who doesn’t do anything unless I’m told, so you can relax.”

“Um, please do your work properly.”

I stopped by this shop every now and then and barely ever saw customers, so I figured it’d be fine.

After hauling a few boxes out of the back room, I was basically done.

The Loli Succubus handled cleaning and organizing the shelves. With no customers, Wiz simply stood behind the counter, smiling politely at the empty air.

Once she ran out of things to do up front, the Loli Succubus disappeared into the back room to clean there too.

Yeah. This really was going to be easy.

“Good day. Oh, is Vanir not in? I see… so he isn’t.”

A slightly rotund man entered, then scanned the shop like he owned the place.

“Welcome. Oh my, thank you again for your help the other day.”

Wiz came out and gave the man a bow. It seems like he’s an acquaintance of hers.

“I’ve brought several items with me today.”

“Er, well… Vanir told me not to buy any more useless products. The staff you sold me the other day—the one that ‘triples the power of any spell’—turned out to be a dud that makes the caster collapse from mana exhaustion after even beginner spells. Vanir got really mad at me for that.”

Oh. So she actually did remember Vanir’s instructions.

Seems like she’d learned after buying so many unsellable items and getting charred by his mysterious beams every time.

“Don’t worry. This one definitely isn’t useless. It’ll be a big hit in town, so Vanir will be happy too. I daresay he’ll even apologize for the way he’s treated you.”

“R-Really. Well, I’ll hear you out, at least.”

“Hold it! Don’t change your mind that easily! And you—don’t take advantage of Vanir being gone to dump your unsellable junk here. Get out!”

“W-What is wrong with you? I came in here with a product that’ll definitely make us both rich.”

“Yeah, it’ll only make you rich.”

I forcibly dragged the protesting man out of the store.

So this was how Wiz kept blowing money on useless goods. Vanir really had his work cut out for him.

“Vanir told you not to buy anything… Where did you even come from?”

Before I noticed it, Aqua was standing by the counter having a chat with Wiz.

She must have slipped in while I was dealing with that man.

“Listen, Wiz! Kazuma told me to stop coming to this store just to hang out!”

“Why would he do that? I’m more than happy to have you as our guest.”

“Right? He said some totally incomprehensible stuff like ‘you’re causing trouble for Vanir, so knock it off already.’”

No, you should understand it.

You do nothing but cause problems in this store.

“Ah, right, that’s not why I came. Listen, I’ve got a brilliant money-making idea—”

“The door is over there. Please go home.”

“Hey! You’ll receive divine punishment for grabbing a goddess by the neck!”

“I’ll tell Kazuma you’re causing trouble for Vanir if you keep this up!”

Aqua put up way more resistance than that merchant earlier, but I eventually got her out the door.

She stopped resisting the moment she heard Kazuma’s name, so she shouldn’t be a problem for the rest of the day.

Wait, wasn’t this supposed to be an easy job? Why was I already exhausted?

Does Sir Vanir have to put up with this every day?

Managing a store sure isn’t easy.

After that, I fended off a few more “enterprising” merchants trying to dump their wares on us.

I even managed to unload a few items onto Yunyun when she came by.

All in all, it was a busy day.

On the bright side, Vanir was in a great mood when he got back and tossed me a few extra eris on top of what we agreed on.

With this, I’ll be able to tide things over until I can take on quests again.

Two days later, I found myself back at the magic item shop.

“Umm, I ran out of money. So do you think I could work here for—”

“The door is over there.”
