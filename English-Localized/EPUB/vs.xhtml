VS!

Fanfic

Translator: yuNS

Localization: Sean92us (Utilizing GPT-5.2)

Occurrence: After Volume 8

Under the watchful eyes of a bunch of kids, Megumin swept her cloak back and announced herself:

“My name is Megumin! Axel’s number-one mage—and the one who wields Explosion magic!”

Standing directly across from her was…

“My name is Yunyun! The number-one mage of the Crimson Demons… Kyaa! Wait, I’m still introducing myself! Stop, stop!!”

Yunyun, who started shrieking after getting sucker-punched mid-introduction.

Early in the morning, at the park in Axel—

Two Crimson Demons were rolling around on the ground.

And I was watching the whole thing.

“…Um. Can we just call that a win for Megumin, for now?”

“I won’t accept that! There’s a rule that says you wait until your opponent finishes introducing themself… Ah—wait, Megumin! I get it, I get it! I lose, I lose! So stop pulling my hair!!”

With Megumin still lunging at her like a rabid cat, Yunyun tearfully admitted defeat.

This whole mess started when Yunyun—who’d come to our mansion for a sleepover—said something offhandedly, with zero bad intentions.

“It’s true I’ve lost more, but that’s because Megumin always decides what the match is. And it’s not like we’ve ever had a proper, legit match, right?”

I listened while Megumin and Yunyun bickered about their history. They went on about all sorts of things…

Like which of them was stronger, and a bunch of other nonsense…

“Sure, I gave you the title of ‘number-one mage of the Crimson Demons,’ but it still annoys me that you can say it out loud with that much pride!”

“That’s not a valid reason to break the rules! Geez, fine, I get it, okay!? Round one is my loss, okay!?”

Megumin pushed Yunyun down and sat on her. No matter how you looked at it, there wasn’t a single trace of maturity here.

“Since Megumin won round one… the next match is Yunyun’s choice, right?”

I’d somehow gotten roped into being their “observer,” and I wanted this embarrassing competition to end as quickly as possible.

Thanks to their over-the-top Crimson Demon-style introductions, we’d turned into a minor attraction for the local kids.

Some of them had even started copying the poses.

With all those kids watching, Yunyun’s eyes glistened a little as she brushed the dirt off her clothes and picked up the purse she’d dropped during the scuffle.

“Then the next match will be… this board game!”

It was a game I recognized—a chess-like board game Megumin was weirdly good at.

“How pathetic. Yunyun, since you don’t have any friends, did you spend the past year training at this game? I’m sure you’re practically a grandmaster by now.”

“You don’t have to say the part about me not making any friends out loud! Anyway, what—are you trying to refuse because you know you’ll lose? A true Crimson Demon takes any challenge head-on, right?”

“You little…”

Yunyun gave Megumin a fearless smile. Megumin growled through clenched teeth and started setting up the pieces.

Yunyun won, and the second round ended in spectacularly boring fashion.

“That makes it one to one! Now then, what should the final match be? A magic showdown like true Crimson Demons? Or, if we’re doing that, we could see who defeats more monsters…”

Having won round two convincingly, Yunyun was uncharacteristically pumped. Which was probably because she almost never got to do things like this with anyone.

Then Megumin turned to me.

“Why don’t we have our observer—Kazuma—choose the final match? I won’t complain about whatever he picks. Now then, let’s finish this!”

“Kazuma will? Well… I’m fine with that…”

Even so, I really wanted to hurry up and go home already…

“Since this is a pain, let’s just settle it with rock-paper—”

“What do you think this sacred showdown between Crimson Demons is supposed to be!?”

“Please pick something more seriously!”

I’d made the only reasonable suggestion possible, and they still tore into me.

Good grief… Maybe I should suggest a “sexy showdown” where the first one to seduce me wins.

As I debated whether to say something that would absolutely get me thrown in jail—

Two patrol officers arrived at the park.

The kids said something to them, and the officers immediately turned to look our way.

…I had a bad feeling about this.

“All right, then. Let’s decide what the match is.”

The officers seemed to have a bad feeling too, because they didn’t take their eyes off Megumin and Yunyun for a second.

“What kind of match are we having? By the way, I was thinking I wouldn’t mind spending today in jail…”

“A-Actually, maybe we can do this some other day, and…”

Seeing the two of them start to chicken out, the officers hurried over and asked,

“You two, can we talk for a moment? We got a report from the neighbors about some people teaching kids weird games…”

“The winner is whoever can stay here the longest! I’m going home first, so—!”

“I’ll take this challenge head-on! Kuh! As expected of Yunyun, to think you’d defeat me so easily—!”

“Hey, I didn’t say I accepted that, so I haven’t won yet! And why is Megumin running away like she’s some tragic heroine!? Ah! Um—Officer, please don’t misunderstand! I wasn’t doing anything bad, I—!”

—Yunyun won.
