What in the world is going on here?

“Big Bro, I want frost lizard steak next!”

“Leave it to me! Big Bro’s super strong! Frost lizard, salamander—whatever you want, it’ll be easy!”

On my way home from school, I found Bukkororii bribing Komekko with food. This is probably something I should report to the village adults…

Then again, this NEET is a rare lifeline for Komekko while she’s still growing, so maybe I could look the other way…

“When I grow up, I’m going to marry Big Bro!”

“You damn NEET—how dare you seduce my sister!”

“W-wait, stop, Megumin! Why are you hitting me all of a sudden?!”

—After hearing his explanation, it turns out Bukkororii found Komekko wandering around the village and decided to keep an eye on her.

If left alone, Komekko probably would’ve wandered into the nearby forest looking for food.

“Listen, Komekko. The forests around the village are full of dangerous monsters, so you can’t go outside the village by yourself.”

Hearing that, Komekko responded.

“Even if I’m with strong Bukkororii?”

“Absolutely not. This NEET might be strong in a fight, but being alone with him is dangerous in a different way.”

“What are you trying to say!? I’m not into that!”

Komekko looked at the two of us as we argued incessantly.

“Who’s stronger, Big Sis or Bukkororii?”

“…”

After a short silence, both of us pointed at ourselves.

“Obviously me—the village’s number-one genius…”

“Obviously me—I spend every day hunting monsters…”

The two of us started squaring off in front of Komekko again.

“…You haven’t forgotten how you cried after I pushed you into the pond when we were little, have you?”

“…How long ago was that? I can do this now, you know!? Light of Reflection!”

“Y-you jerk, how could you use magic in a fight with a girl younger than you!?”

I shouted angrily at Bukkororii as he hid himself with magic, and his smug voice called back from somewhere nearby.

“Admit your defeat, Megumin! I’m not the same guy I was back then! Even if I had to face the Demon King’s Army…”

“- Oh, isn’t that Megumin and Komekko?”

The one who interrupted Bukkororii mid-sentence was the owner of the village’s number-one shoe shop. In other words, Bukkororii’s father.

“I told him to mind the store, and he went and disappeared somewhere. Have you two seen that wastrel?”

Komekko and I pointed at empty air.

“He should be right around there.”

As we said that, we heard frantic footsteps as someone bolted away.

And Bukkororii’s father cast a spell in that direction—
