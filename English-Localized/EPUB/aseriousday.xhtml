Dust Spinoff 3 Short Story: A Serious Day

Translator: Cannongerbil

Editors: Ulti, Xenthur

Localization: Sean92us (Utilizing GPT-5.2)

Occurrence: After Dust Spinoff Volume 3

“I think I’ll go on an adventure.”

I announced it while we were eating at the guild.

My companions froze for a moment, then went right back to eating after giving me the briefest glance.

I’m not so easily discouraged. I stared at the ceiling, grabbed the hilt of my sword, and launched into a passionate speech.

“I don’t have any money. Normally, I’d laze around and brainstorm ways to make it the easy way. But I recently had a revelation. If I want money, all I have to do is work for it.”

I thought that was pretty good, personally.

A little worried by their silence, I lowered my gaze… only to find all three of them quietly eating.

“…Hey, aren’t you going to say something? ’Well said,‘ or ’I’m actually moved,’ or anything like that? You can praise me, you know.”

In response, Lynn said, exasperated,

“Aren’t you just stating the obvious?”

“Let me guess—you saw a woman taking care of a rain-soaked cat, fell for her, and decided you needed to improve your standing so you can woo her. Something that cliché, right?”

“Then just work seriously from now on.”

These guys… They saw right through my perfect strategy!

I tried using one of those “raise affection” tips I’d read in a book at the general store, but it didn’t work at all.

Dammit. It’s because you stock useless books like that your store is always empty. That old man should take his work more seriously.

“All right, then—let’s go on an adventure.”

“Why are you deciding that by yourself? Before we talk about going anywhere, do we even have a quest?”

“Yeah. I found a quest earlier that really caught my eye, so I grabbed it.”

With that, I enthusiastically slapped the request form onto the table.

After reading it, my companions looked up at me with dubious expressions.

“What’s with this quest?”

“It’s exactly what it says. Capture a giant toad alive. With our party, it shouldn’t be that hard.”

“Sure, it’s doable, but… Dust, what is anyone supposed to do with a live giant toad?”

Keith tapped the request form with a finger, looking fed up.

Taylor didn’t say anything, but he nodded along. He seemed to agree.

“That got me curious too, so I asked around about who posted it. Turns out the request came from a certain noble lady.”

“A noble lady? What would someone like that want with a live giant toad?”

“Maybe she wants to try commoner food? But if that were the case, why request it alive?”

“Hmm… Maybe she’s picky about ingredient freshness?”

My companions started tossing ideas around.

I was just as confused at first, but everything clicked the moment the guild gave me the requestor’s name.

“Yeah, you’d think so, right? But what if I told you that noble lady was Lalatina?”

“Ah…”

“Why would Darkness…” x2

Lynn got it instantly, but Taylor and Keith still looked confused.

Come to think of it, Lynn had experienced Darkness’s… peculiarities firsthand, but Taylor and Keith hadn’t seen her in action yet.

“Well, there are a lot of uses for one.”

I wouldn’t have minded enlightening them, but Taylor really did look up to Darkness as a fellow crusader. There was no urgent need to shatter his image of her.

Plenty of adventurers still didn’t know her true nature. They’d probably sink into despair if they found out.

“Darkness probably won’t stiff us on the pay, but before we go out and grab one… wouldn’t it be better to talk to Kazuma first?”

“As long as we get paid, what’s the problem? Why do we need to run everything by my friend?”

“The part where she wants a live monster brought to her gives me a really bad feeling.”

Actually, yeah. I hadn’t thought too hard about it because she’s a pervert, but… what was she planning to do once she had a live giant toad?

The last time I saw her fight, she threw off her armor and charged straight into a horde of goblins…

“…Yeah, maybe we’d better run this by Kazuma.”

“Right? It’s not like we lose anything if it’s nothing. Kazuma’s the leader of that party, after all.”

“All right. We’ll head over to Kazuma’s place after lunch.”

When we met up with Kazuma, it sounded like this was the first he’d heard of the request, so he called Darkness out and gave her a lecture.

“It’s not because I’m jealous that you guys are the only ones who get eaten! I just want to get all slimy with that saliva…”

She tried to keep a straight face while coming up with a bunch of incomprehensible excuses, but Kazuma’s scolding only seemed to excite her more.

She was absolutely beyond help.

The quest got pulled, but we still received a cancellation fee. I guess taking life seriously once in a while isn’t so bad.
