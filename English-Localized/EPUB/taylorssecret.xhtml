Dust Spinoff 2 Short Story: Taylor’s Secret

Translator: Cannongerbil

Editors: Xenthur, Ulti

Localization: Sean92us (Utilizing GPT-5.2)

Occurrence: After Dust Spinoff Volume 2

“Hey, Dust. Can I ask you something?”

Lynn called out to me over dinner at our usual table in the guild hall.

“If you’re going to ask me what my type is, I ain’t answering.”

“Not that. Don’t you think Keith and Taylor have been acting kind of weird lately?”

Here I was, worried about what had her frowning so hard. So it was them.

I hadn’t noticed anything.

“Weird how?”

“You hang out with Keith a lot. Has he been doing anything shady? The other day, when I called out to both of you, he panicked.”

“Did that happen? …I don’t remember.”

The only thing that comes to mind is… that time we ran into Lynn on our way back from the Succubus Shop.

That place is a secret known only to male adventurers. Even if it’s Lynn, there’s no way we can let her find out.

“Plus, he drinks every day. But lately, even when he has money, there are times when he doesn’t. Same goes for you.”

Yeah, because we were saving up for the best dreams money can buy.

“We, uh… realized the value of having a healthy body. You’ve got to take a break now and then and let your liver rest, right?”

“…”

Don’t just stare at me like that. Say something.

She didn’t believe me for a second. Honestly, I don’t blame her. It was a terrible lie.

“Fine. I’ll ask you for the details later. The real problem is Taylor.”

“He doesn’t really come out with us.”

We kept the Succubus Shop a secret from Taylor.

He’s stubborn in that uptight way, and I could easily see him lecturing us instead of using it himself.

“I’d worry less if he was with you. But every now and then, Taylor meets someone in secret. A friend told me he keeps going to a high-class bar—with someone.”

“Wait, what? He has a girl?! Unforgivable!”

And here I thought he had no luck with women! That traitor!

…Still, I couldn’t remember him ever showing signs of that.

“That’s the thing. It’s not a girl. He’s meeting with a man.”

“Then it’s just a friend, right? Leave it alone. It’s not our business. As long as he’s not doing something illegal, don’t pry.”

“And who was the one who stuck their nose in when that noble asked me for advice?”

“Friends are important. Let’s keep an eye on him. What if he’s getting mixed up with some unsavory people?!”

Lynn narrowed her eyes even further as I boldly proclaimed that.

I got dragged into an absolute nightmare last time I roped Kazuma into helping me with photos, so this time, we’d watch from a safe distance.

Two days later.

After a quest, Keith invited everyone to cool off at the bar, but Taylor refused.

“Sorry. I’ve got plans with someone.”

With that, he left the guild without even eating dinner.

Lynn and I turned Keith down too, then followed Taylor.

At the fancy bar Lynn had heard about, Taylor walked in without even glancing around.

Lynn and I went in after him with our hoods pulled low.

The place was way classier than the guild hall. We spotted Taylor and took the seats behind him.

“This really looks like a date with a woman.”

“I don’t think so. The person sitting across from Taylor is definitely a guy. I saw him when we sat down. I just couldn’t make out his face…”

Lynn and I whispered as we settled in.

“Sorry I’m late.”

“No worries. I just got here myself.”

I’d heard that voice before.

It was a little muffled from where I sat, so I couldn’t be sure, but… that voice…

“How was your day?”

“No major issues. Keith and Dust don’t have any problems when it comes to ability, and Lynn’s always reliable.”

Lynn, clearly thrilled at the praise, jabbed me in the side with her elbow.

“Ugh, I’m jealous. Mine was absolutely horrible. The ones on my side don’t listen to a word I say.”

“I-I see. I’ve never teamed up with them, so I don’t know firsthand what you’re dealing with, but… Dust has told me plenty.”

Yeah. The guy Taylor was talking to was definitely Kazuma.

“That’s Kazuma, right? I didn’t know they were that close. Do you think they feel some kind of kinship from being party leaders?”

“Maybe. Still, there’s no reason they’d need to keep this secret.”

Two guys grabbing a drink together isn’t exactly scandalous.

And here I was expecting something way worse.

…Of course, the moment I thought that, they started complaining nonstop about their party members.

Kazuma had plenty of complaints, and honestly, I agreed with most of them. Taylor didn’t seem to have any complaints about Lynn, but…

“Aren’t most of his complaints about me?”

“He’s not wrong… Oh, I get it. They’re sneaking around because they don’t want their parties to find out they’re venting.”

…Let’s just go home.
